Presentation of the system purpose:
This CS describes the requirements applicable to every Radars2026 system developed within the framework of a vehicle project of Stellantis.

The Radars2026 is a forward-looking camera that analyses the environment of the host vehicle and broadcast on CAN or ethernet (option) networks data about and decisions based on this environment

The Radars2026 are components of the ADAS vehicle system which contributes to reduce the number of traffic accidents by alerting the driver and/or actuating other systems of the vehicle (steering system, braking system…)

The Radars2026 can be in charge of:
-responsible to fulfill features required in [FEA00], fusion based on the front camera RADARS2026
-sending raw point cloud on ethernet
-sending processed data on CAN FD
EE& functional architecture to converge during the RFQ

In this document, the front radar is called FrontRadar2026 or FR2026, the corner radars are called CornerRadars2026 or CR2026, requirement applicable to both components are called Radars2026.

[Heading 3] DEPENDABILITY VOCABULARY

Safety: Capacity of the system to prevent the appearance, under the given conditions, of critical or catastrophic events.

The Standard ISO 26262 declines for the vehicle, the standard CEI 61508 that refers to the safety of the electrical and electronic systems; an initial version has been published in 2008, the final text will be published in 2010.

Availability: Capacity of the system to perform or to be able to perform a required function at a given moment.

Reliability: Capacity of a device to perform a required function, in given conditions of use and maintenance, for a given duration (NF X 60-500).

Unreliability: Probability of being defective before a given time, mileage or number of stresses. Unreliability according to a given duration t is the probability of failure in the interval[0, t]:


Durability: Capacity of a good to perform a function until a limit state is reached (NF X 60-500)

Performance of a Radars2026 (or part of a system): This is a matter of the behaviour performances of the Radars2026 or sub-system used by the customer. These performances result from the intrinsic resistance of the Radars2026s with their dispersion and the forces levels encountered in customer use.
Note: The supplier brings his view feedback when he has it, but the performance in service is always set by PSA.

Maintainability: In given conditions of use for which it was designed, the capacity of a system to be maintained or restored to a state where it can perform a required function, when the maintenance is performed in given conditions, using the prescribed procedures and resources.

Failure: This is the behaviour that leads to a non-desired situation.
The vocabulary of the standard ISO26262 distinguishes between:
“random” failure: which has a physical character that can be altered by a reliability strategy (be it: Weibull, normal, exponential…) 
a failure triggered by an external environment itself random is part of this category of breakdowns, if the environment conditions were planned at the moment of the design.
“Systematic” failure: resulting from the non-conformity of the state of the art at the level of design, validation, production... (ex: specification, traceability of requirements, lack of knowledge on the maximum stress) 
The development of software without respecting the V cycle is a systematic failure. 
The systematic failures are not part of the perimeter discussed by the durability procedure.
Failure mode: Physical phenomenon at the origin of the failure

Failure modes: This is a matter of the degradation process that leads to failure after a certain time of use of the vehicle by the customer. 
There are:
the fatigue phenomena:
the fatigue under variable load: is generally subject to a PSA specification of the level of “equivalent fatigue” as a resistance to a number of cycles set. The intrinsic resistance under this number of cycles follows a normal strategy.
the fatigue under approximately constant load: is the number of rupture cycles which is specified; according to the level of knowledge of the behaviour in fatigue, or we can also use a otentia via the Wöhler curves, or we can model the resistance distribution by a log-normal strategy.
The fatigue damage level is not measurable in the vehicle: the intrinsic resistance potential can only be evaluated only by tests carried out until rupture (the experience feedback allows us in some cases to avoid it).

wear: the number of cycles with rupture is otenti by Weibull distribution. The wear level can generally be measured, which allows us to estimate the intrinsic resistance potential by extrapolation if we know the maximum wear level leading to the loss of service.

failures modelled by an exponential strategy (random or systematic in conformity with the standard) characterized by an instantaneous failure rate or an average time (distance or stress) between failures. 


Steps should be taken to adjust the phenomena of corrosion or degradation in ambient air (ozone for example). The impact of these phenomena is estimated by the SPR as a natural accelerator factor and is communicated to PSA when identified.

We retain the main failure modes which correspond to the weak links. This means that the other failure modes are (subject to a normal implementation) manifestly impossible to encounter taking into account the dimensioning and the layout of the studied system.

Dreaded event (DE): This is the event after a failure having a negative impact on the customer or his environment. There is the functional ER caused by the failure of a functional output flow of the “threat – damage ER” which is not caused by a failure of the Radars2026’s or the system’s functional.

The dreaded events are classified according to their criticality level: The criticality level is an estimate of the effect of the dreaded event considered in terms of inconvenience for the user, of cost for repairing, or of individual risk. ASIL Level: This is the safety integrity level “ASIL” (Automotive Safety Integrity Level) for each safety function or system. This is a scale with 5 levels of growing criticality (QM, A, B, C, D) that refines the notion of ER G4. The QM level refers to the Quality Management and to the standard ISO-TS 16949. The level results from an analysis that includes:
- The frequency of the risk situations (E1 to E4)
- The driver’s ability to control the consequences of the ER (C0 to C3)
- The severity of the consequences (S1 to S3)

Acceleration factor: Its application increases the damage and accelerates the failure of the RADARS2026 or sub-system. There is a major interest in accelerating the durability tests.
Note: Such a factor is used if the SPR commits himself on the value of the acceleration coefficient (recognized calculation, experimentation, etc.).

Aggravated Short-Circuit (CCA): One is in the presence of CCA when the presence of flames or combustion is not confined to the inside of the equipment and/or when a voluntary intervention is necessary to stop it.
There are the Parallel CCA and the Series CCA:
Parallel CC: Insulation fault at constant voltage between + and – (potential difference). If the insulation resistance decreases, the leakage current increases the power increases and a hot spot (P= U²/R) is created
Series CC: Rated current or overload conduction fault. If the electrical resistance increases the power increases and a hot spot (P= RI²) is created.


1st barrier: All the technical solutions that prevent the appearance of a “hot spot” in the EE part (critical temperature rise in relation to its environment)

2nd barrier: All the technical solutions that prevent the propagation of the hot spot outside the EE part.
